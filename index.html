Nauvoo Family Adventure

A faith-filled, kid-friendly Midwest trip with pageants, church history, BBQ, the Mississippi River, and St. Louis fun.

Why this trip is worth it

This is not just a “sit through church history” trip. It mixes meaningful places with kid-friendly vacation moments: pageants, hotel pools, BBQ, the Mississippi River, City Museum, the zoo, and family photos at the Nauvoo Temple.

Pageants: Free major events
Hotels: Comfortable but budget-aware
Kids: Built-in fun and downtime
Memory: A trip they’ll actually remember

The big wins

Nauvoo & British Pageants

Two free evening pageants with the Country Fair beforehand.

Carthage Jail

The most powerful church history stop near Nauvoo.

Mississippi River Cruise

A vacation-feeling activity with views of the river and Arch.

Day-by-day plan

Thu, July 23

Arrive in St. Louis

8 AM–2 PM

Fly SLC → STL, pick up rental car, check into Drury Plaza Hotel at the Arch.

2 PM–5 PM

Gateway Arch grounds and riverfront walk. Optional tram if everyone has energy. Gateway Arch details.

5:30 PM–8 PM

Dinner at Sugarfire Smoke House, then optional Mississippi River cruise.

Fri, July 24

City Museum + British Pageant

9 AM–1 PM

City Museum — probably the best kid activity in St. Louis. Giant climbable art/playground/museum.

1:30 PM–5:30 PM

Drive toward Nauvoo. Check into a Burlington or Fort Madison area hotel.

7 PM–10 PM

Country Fair + British Pageant. Bring snacks, bug spray, small blankets, and low expectations for perfect kid behavior.

Sat, July 25

Carthage + Nauvoo Pageant

9 AM–11 AM

Carthage Jail. This is the “don’t miss” church history stop.

11:30 AM–4 PM

Temple grounds, family photos, bakery, wagon ride, blacksmith, brick yard, and pool break.

7 PM–10 PM

Country Fair + Nauvoo Pageant. This is the spiritual centerpiece of the trip.

Sun, July 26

Slow morning + return to St. Louis

Morning

Church or relaxed morning. Breakfast, pack, and one last Nauvoo photo stop.

Late morning–4 PM

Drive back toward St. Louis. Optional minor league baseball detour if timing works.

Evening

Check back into Drury Plaza. Pool night, easy dinner, or Salt + Smoke.

Mon, July 27

Zoo + fly home

8 AM–12 PM

St. Louis Zoo — free admission and a great final activity before flying home.

12 PM–2 PM

Lunch, return rental car, airport.

Afternoon/evening

Fly home with tired kids and a lot of memories.

Hotels

St. Louis: Drury Plaza Hotel at the Arch. Best mix of location, breakfast, evening food, pool, and value.

Nauvoo area: Prefer Burlington or Fort Madison over older Nauvoo hotels. Look for Hampton Inn, Holiday Inn Express, or Cobblestone-style options under $200/night.

Food wins

Pappy’s Smokehouse — famous BBQ, best as lunch.

Sugarfire — easiest family BBQ option.

Salt + Smoke — good sit-down dinner option.

Estimated budget

Flights$1,200–$1,800
Hotels$700–$900
Rental car$300–$450
Food + activities$600–$850
Total estimate$2,800–$4,000

The pageants are free, the zoo is free, and Drury’s breakfast/evening food can save real money.

The Amy pitch

This trip gives us something meaningful without Disney-level spending. It has church history, big kid energy, family photos, hotel pools, BBQ, and the Mississippi River. It is short enough to be manageable, but full enough to feel like a real family vacation.
